Tutor Helper Mobile App

The project was made within the Project Management class by a team of 4 members. I was mostly responsible for the UX process. Each week we switched a project manager role.

Type

Short-term project

Role

UI/UX Designer, UX Researcher, sometimes Manager

Timeline

Jan-Mar 2018

The product was an application for students at Seattle Central College to schedule appointments with tutors on campus.

We established a work scope for the quarter, conducted user research and competitive analysis, created a user journey map, established an MVP, and a marketing plan, and designed the first version of the app.

Personas

Katarina

  • Full-time student. Needs help with classes.
  • Schedules appointments in person because the current college app doesn't work properly.
  • Had a bad experience with tutoring: tutor didn't know how to help, session time wasn't enough, once the tutor didn't show up.

Erica

  • Part-time math tutor at Seattle Central College. Loves to share knowledge with students.
  • Works 4 hours 2-3 days a week.
  • Doesn't know her appointments for the day because there's no place online to check it out.

User story map — Katarina

The first version of the app was focused on Katarina-the-student. Erica-the-tutor was only supposed to get email notifications about scheduled sessions.

Solution

Katarina had 2 key problems with the existed application: difficult scheduling and not helpful tutoring.

Difficult scheduling

Our app made the scheduling process much easier—just 3 steps: choose type of study, choose time+tutor, and confirm.

Tutoring is not helpful

It happened for 2 reasons: 1) tutors were not qualified for certain tasks, 2) there was not enough session time.

To make sure that a tutor was qualified for the task, our app showed tutor's qualifications so that Katarina could choose the right one.

To make the session more productive, Katarina was able to leave a comment for the tutor about her task. That way the tutor got a chance to prepare.
