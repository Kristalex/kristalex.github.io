Tutor Helper mobile app

The project was made within Project Management class by a team of 4 members. I was mostly responsible for the UX process. Each week we switched a project manager role.

Type

College project

Role

UI/UX Designer, UX Researcher, sometimes Manager

Timeline

Jan-Mar 2018

The product is an application for students at Seattle Central College to schedule appointments with tutors on campus.

We established a work scope for the quarter, conducted user research and competitive analysis, created a user journey map, MVP, marketing plan, and the first design prototypes of the app.

Personas and problems

Katarina

  • Full-time student. Needs help with classes.
  • Schedules appointments in person because a college app doesn't work properly.
  • Had a bad experience with tutoring: tutor didn't know how to help, session time wasn't enough, once the tutor didn't show up.

Erica

  • Part-time math tutor at Seattle Central College. Loves to share knowledge with students.
  • Works 4 hours 2-3 days a week.
  • Doesn't know her appointments for the day because there's no place online to check it out.

User story map

Solution

The first version of the app is for Katarina. It solves 2 main problems:

  1. Difficult scheduling
    The application makes this process much easier—just 3 steps: choose type of study, choose time+tutor, and confirm.
  2. Tutors are not helpful: don't have necessary qualifications, not enough session time
    The app shows info about tutor's qualifications so that Katarina could choose the right tutor. Then, Katarina can leave a comment for a tutor about her task so that a tutor get a chance to prepare.
