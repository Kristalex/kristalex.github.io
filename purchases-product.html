Contracting Search Web App

I led this project from the initial vision. I helped in conducting user and competitive research, analyzing data, defining user personas and MVP. Based on research, I iteratively created user flows, wireframes, interactive prototypes, and later visual design.

Product website Mobile version

Type

Long-term project

Role

UI/UX Designer

Timeline

Jan 2013–Mar 2018

Market

In Russia, when companies need services, they place their needs on special websites as auctions. Other companies, that can provide services, search for those auctions to participate in them and win the contract.

We call the first companies customers, and the second — suppliers.

Mission

Our product aggregates auctions from different sources and provides a search for suppliers. Along with the search, the product helps users organize and analyze potential auctions.

Competitors

  • Free government website
  • Some paid websites with the same goal

In design and implementation of every feature, we followed UX best practices. Our strategy was not based on first to market, but best to market. The product is still growing and already has 18k+ paid users.

User flow

User flow can be divided into 3 steps: search, analysis, and participation. On each step of the flow, we uncovered user problems and tried to solve them. Here's the user flow in our service.

Solution

STEP 1. SEARCH

After the research of how our users were searching on the government website, we revealed 3 key problems:

  1. Difficult search form
  2. Too many irrelevant results
  3. Repetitive search

For the first version of the search form, I've only designed two fields that covered 90% of user scenarios: “Keywords” and “Region”. Every new search option added after was carefully researched and tested to keep make sure that it is something users really need.

To make search results more relevant I partnered with developers to work on a search engine that is possible to develop and that supports user scenarios.

To stop users from repeating their search requests every day, I designed “Saved searches”. Now users save their frequent requests and get notifications by email about new results. I worked on both website and emails design.

STEP 2. ANALYSIS

After the search, users analyzed potential auctions: if it matches their company's services and if it's worth participating.

On this step, users had 2 key problems:

  1. Auction data was too difficult to understand because of the sophisticated legal jargon and overlapping content.
  2. Users couldn't check if they could trust customers.

To simplify auction data, I worked closely with a Business Analyst. We changed labels and organized data in a more clear and logical way. I analyzed data from different sources and created the same view format for it. That allowed users to understand information without external support and to decrease the decision making time.

To allow suppliers to check customers and to uncover a participants affiliation, we learned what data can show that kind of information, developers found a way to get this data, and I created the whole user flow and design. A lot of our competitors showed complicated analytics with similar data but we learned that users didn't understand it and couldn't apply to their business. My goal was to make the data simple and useful.

STEP 3. PREPARATION FOR PARTICIPATION

When users found and analyzed potential auctions, they were ready to work closer with them.

On this step, users were doing a lot of manual work: they copied data from the website by hand, created Excel files, and combined it all into emails for sharing with their coworkers. We created an online storage “Favorites” to make work process and work collaboration easier.

However, the real problem was that customers often changed auction conditions and there wasn't any way to learn about it. In such situations, suppliers didn't know they needed to change their application documents and failed. To solve this problem, we started to notify users about customers updates.

PROMO & INFO PART

Apart from helping our users search and work with auctions, we educate them with articles, instructions, and experts help and keep updated on the legislation with news. Here I worked on the information architecture, user flow, wireframes, and user communication.

The application looks like one whole but in fact there are two different websites united together and two different teams working on them. I managed collaboration on integration to create a flawless user flow so that users wouldn't be confused by two different websites.
