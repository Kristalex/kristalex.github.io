Contracting Search Web App

The product is a web application for suppliers to search for and work on auctions placed by customers who need services or goods. It's focused on b2g and b2b markets in Russia. The app aggregates auctions from different sources and provides a search, analysis, and management system for suppliers.

Product website

Type

Long-term project, web application

Role

UI/UX Designer

Timeline

Jan 2013–Mar 2018

What I did

I led this project from the initial vision to 18k+ paid users. I helped in conducting user and competitive research, analyzing data, defining user personas and an MVP. Based on research, I iteratively created user flows, wireframes, interactive prototypes, and visual design.

User Research

We started research with usability testings on the existing government website way before the first wireframes of our own. After around 10 testings and contextual interviews we had a pretty good idea of what was going on in the field and what problems users were dealing with.

We divided the whole process of participating in auctions into 3 major steps: search, analysis, and actual participation. Based on research, we saw that users were hugely suffering even on the first step. So we decided to focus our MVP on the first step only.

Later on, we were covering other user steps of the process iteratively researching, implementing, and testing the product.

Here's just a short preview of our personas who we were designing for.

Problem — Solution

User step 1 — Search

Problem “Results”
Too many irrelevant results.

Solution
The final solution was search results sorted by a publish date with an opportunity to sort differently depending on a scenario.

Process
Working with developers on the search engine, I had to compromise in a way that users would still have decent experience and developers would get some more time to improve search algorithms.

Problem “Search form”
The search process wasn't easy: difficult search form and many irrelevant results.

Solution
The first version of a search form was with only two fields that covered 90% of user scenarios: “Keywords” and “Region”. Then step by step we added new search options as an advanced search. Every new option was carefully researched to make sure users really needed it.

Problem “Repetitive search”
Users had to repeat their search every single time.

Solution
We designed “Saved searches” to allow users to save regular requests and get notifications by email about new results.

User step 2 — Analysis of potential auctions

Problem “Data”
Auction data was too difficult to understand because of the sophisticated legal jargon and overlapping content. Users spent a lot of time on decision making and often needed external support.

Solution
Working closely with a Business Analyst, we unified auction data from different sources and simplified it.

Problem “Customers & competitors”
Users couldn't check if they could trust customers.

Solution
I designed the whole flow for users to check customers and uncover a participants affiliation.

Insight ♥
A lot of our competitors showed complicated analytics with similar data but we learned that users didn't understand it and couldn't apply to their business. So we dug deep to make analytics valuable.

User step 3 — Preparation for participation in auctions

Problem “Manual work”
Users did a lot of manual work: they copied data from the website by hand, created Excel files, and combined it all into emails to share with their coworkers.

Solution
We created an online storage “Favorites” to make the work process and collaboration easier.

Problem “Changing conditions”
Customers often changed auction conditions and there wasn't any way to learn about it. In such situations, suppliers didn't know they needed to change their application documents and failed the auctions.

Solution
We found a way to learn about changes and started to notify users about customers' updates.

Mobile version

After we subscribed our user to emails, we saw that 60% of openings were on mobile phones. Users went to view auctions from emails and had a terrible experience because the website was not mobile-friendly. Therefore, we created a mobile version for the screen with the list of auctions first and then for the whole site.

Education

Another important part of the project was making the whole auction process look easy. We simplified the legislation in our articles and kept users updated on constantly changing government rules.

To do that, we created “News and instructions” section on our website which in fact was a whole other website controlled by another team.

My role was to collaborate on integration and to create a flawless user flow so that users wouldn't be confused by two different websites. I worked on the information architecture, user flow, wireframes, user communication, articles, and emails.
