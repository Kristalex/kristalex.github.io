Searching for purchases

Searching by parameters is the first step for Dmitry and Irina. They work with a search form to make requests for their company.

Short search form and results

We tested users and researched competitors to find out which search fields are the most important for our users.

Search results contain information essential for a decision to go deeper or not.

Additional filters
Only after the key scenarios did we pay attention to less frequent cases. You can read about how the form has changed over time in Search form's lifetime.

After the first time, Dmitry and Irina save their searches and subscribe to them. Since then, they will receive new purchases by email every day which fastens the search process.

Saving and subscribing

To connect saved searches and subscription transparently, I made a continuous process—one control to make it step by step.

UX testings showed that it was much easier to subscribe right after saving a search.

Analytics showed that the amount of subscribed users increased considerably.

Since users saved searches they can search quicker.


After the subscription, we saw that 60% of our users opened emails on their mobile phones. From there they went to view purchases and had a terrible experience because the website was not mobile-friendly. Therefore, starting with making the list of purchases mobile-friendly, we made a mobile version for the whole site.

Analyzing

Dmitry and Irina decide here whether a purchase is convenient for their company. If it is, they mark it as favourite. Dmitry's work is done at this moment. Irina will come back to the purchase page to prepare documents. She will analyze a customer before participation to eliminate risks when the customer doesn't pay or always chooses the same suppliers. After participation, Irina will analyze the finished purchase to improve her strategy.

Purchase's data

There was lots of information taken from different sources in different formats. I showed the most important data using the same view to shorten the time needed for a decision.


Analyzing customer

A supplier can analyze a customer by their previous auctions: see competitors, who wins more often, if there's a fair competition.

It's a way to uncover an affiliation of participants, conditions on what participants play, and a percentage of price reducing.


Preparing for participation

After adding purchases to favourites, Dmitry delegates preparation to other specialists. Meanwhile, Irina starts to prepare for participation by herself. In favourites, she will also get notifications about customer's changes in purchases.
