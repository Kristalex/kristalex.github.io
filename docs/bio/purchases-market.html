Market, mission, problems

Market

In Russia, if companies need to buy goods or services, they place their needs on special websites. We call these needs purchases. Suppliers search for purchases to provide services.

Mission

We aggregate purchases and help suppliers to find them. After searching, we help to organize and analyse purchases.

Competitors

  • a free government website
  • a number of paid websites with the same goal

We bet on a good search and usability. In design and implementation of every feature, we followed UX best practices. Our strategy was not based on first to market, but best to market. And it paid of. The product is still growing and already has over 10000 users.

Problems we solved

  1. Before: Users couldn't access purchases because the government website was often unavailable.
    Now: We create a reliable system.
  2. Before: Users couldn't find anything because there was a lot of irrelevant search results on the government website.
    Now: Our search engine is much more efficient.
  3. Before: Users spent about 20 minutes a day on composing search requests.
    Now: They can save regular requests and get purchases every morning by email automatically.
  4. Before: Users went to special courses to study what all information means and how to work on the government website.
    Now: We simplify scenarios, omit unnecessary information, make a purchase page readable and concise.
  5. Before: Users had to create an offline storage for purchases to continue to work with them.
    Now: They have an online storage for interesting purchases—Favourites.
  6. Before: Users didn't know when something changes in their purchases—it could be a reason to fail participation.
    Now: We monitor changes in favourite purchases and notify users about it.
  7. Before: Users could loose their money by choosing customers who don’t pay properly or have always the same winners.
    Now: There's a page for every company with its participation data to analyze.
