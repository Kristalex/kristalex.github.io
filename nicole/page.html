Search Engine Optimization

Search Engine Optimisation is a set of tools that helps the website to be higher on search result pages

Purpose

SEO is about creating a good online presence and making yourself visible to users, making your link more likely to get clicked. Once you have achieved a higher ranking, more people will see you as an authority in your field and it becomes easier to create a good customer base.

Selection of keywords

A good way to start is by making a list of relevant keywords related to your business and plug each one into a Google search and see what happens. To test that theory, Jon Rognerud from entreprenuer.com wrote up a checklist of 4 steps to get you started.

Begin with this: What would you type into a search engine to find your business website or page? Then, ask your customers!..

Determine an indicative number of recent search engine queries and how many websites are competing for each keyword.

Jon Rognerud, SEO Expert

By determining who is competing for your selected keywords, you are able to research your competition and find words that describe the intent and theme of your content.

Title tags

After you do your keywords research, you want to place 3-5 of them into the meta tags section in the HTML code of your page. According to WordStream, “There are 4 different types of meta tags - meta keywords attribute, title tag, meta description attribute and the meta robots attribute (WordStream article)”.

Keywords meta tag

They suggest that the keywords attribute is old news because Google doesn’t use them in their ranking algorithm anymore. Simply put, internet marketers would insert keywords that were unrelated to their content into their source code in attempt to get more page hits to their site. This would steal traffic from sites that actually were about the content keywords so Google got smart and decided to devalue the tool.

Title meta tag

The folks at Wordstream stress the importance of the title tag as a means for your keywords to get the most successful SEO outcome. Title tags have a real impact on search engine because these titles are visible to the average user.

What to write

When you place keywords in the meta tags section of your HTML code, you want words that reflect the intent and theme of the page. Using phrases is also helpful and will allow search engines to quickly determine what the page is about.

To take a closer look into these search engine rankings for your chosen keywords, do some tests and record your findings in a spreadsheet. It is also a good idea to refresh your keywords and add new ones from time to time to improve search engine rankings.

SEO or PPC

As a business your biggest concern is converting online traffic and clicks into sales. If you are on the top of Search Engine Results Pages, people are more likely to visit your website. There are two ways to get there—SEO and PPC.

People's trust

Research shows that sites that use SEO have a higher probability of getting visited than those that use PPC (pay-per-click). This is because more people trust the organic result more than the top result which is payed for to get a higher ranking.

However, this depends on the business area. For example, research shows that with searches related to electronics and computers, the user is less likely to click on the first three search results than a user searching for sites related to finance and insurance.

Time and money

PPC-advertising works instantly but can get expensive fast—the more you pay, the higher the ranking will be. SEO takes week or months but you don’t have to pay for traffic. Although, most companies still have to hire professionals to create their websites and help them with SEO.

Up-to-date

There is also another concern which SEO-users need to be aware about. With search engines constantly working to update and improve their algorithms, that also increases the risk of your SEO-page to lose its ranking, if it is not up to date with the changes. A lot of hard work can get wiped out very quickly, and by using PPC you can ensure that your site keeps its place in the SERP.
